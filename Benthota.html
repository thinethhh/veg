Benthota turtle hatchery
Southern province, Galle

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project, a renowned destination in Bentota, attracts travelers of all kinds. Our island's pristine golden shores not only captivate visitors but also serve as a vital nesting habitat for various endangered turtle species, including the Olive Ridley and Leatherback turtles. Focusing on conservation and protection, the project strives to safeguard these precious eggs from predators and other threats. A visit to the turtle hatchery in Bentota offers a unique opportunity to acquire knowledge about these magnificent and endangered sea creatures. Explore and discover the grace and beauty of these turtles while supporting their conservation efforts at our esteemed turtle hatchery in Bentota.

CONVERSATION EFFORTS


  • The Turtle Hatchery protects and incubates sea turtle eggs until they hatch and are ready to return to the ocean.
  • The project rescues and rehabilitates injured sea turtles affected by fishing nets, boat motors, and other dangers encountered at sea.
  • Rehabilitated turtles are released back into their natural habitat, promoting their survival and contributing to sea turtle conservation efforts.
