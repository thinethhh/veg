Kosgoda turtle hatchery
Southern province, Galle

Established in 1978, this hatchery holds the distinction of being Sri Lanka's first Turtle Hatchery, thanks to the visionary efforts of Dr. Upen de Silva and Dr. R. S. B Wickremsinghe. Our unwavering commitment spans a lifetime, aiming to promote environmental sustainability and safeguard the country's wildlife and their habitats, with a particular focus on endangered species. Our ultimate objective is to ensure a greener future by actively leading, facilitating, and inspiring individuals to contribute to the protection, conservation, and enhancement of the environment, including the preservation of national habitats, flora, and fauna. We strive to motivate people to take proactive measures for the conservation of biodiversity and the natural environment in every possible way.

CONVERSATION EFFORTS


  • Established in 1978, the Victor Hasselblad Sea Turtle Research and Conservation Center in Kosgoda is Sri Lanka's first turtle conservation facility. It holds valuable records of the White Sea turtle and attracts volunteers worldwide.
  • With international recognition, the center offers a unique opportunity for volunteers to contribute to sea turtle research and conservation.
  • The center boasts a rich history, with extensive archives documenting sea turtle tagging, healing, and rescue efforts. It holds the impressive record of releasing over 4.5 million turtles back into their natural habitat. Visitors can also enjoy the beauty of the clean and picturesque beach.
